What Happens Next

A simple path from inquiry to proposal discussion

Most projects do not need a long discovery phase at the start. A clear first RFQ is usually enough to move into practical technical review.

01

Submit project details

Send the waste type, target capacity, and plant limits so the team can understand the operating context.

02

Engineering review

WEI LAN reviews material type, likely process modules, and the overall project direction before discussing configuration.

03

System recommendation

The team recommends the most suitable product line, process path, and configuration range for deeper evaluation.

04

Proposal and quotation discussion

Once the basic fit is clear, the conversation can move into documents, parameters, and commercial discussion.

Inquiry Checklist

The four inputs that improve proposal quality fastest

Even a short first message becomes much more actionable when it includes the operational basics below.

Input 01

Waste type and composition

Clarify whether the material is PET, HDPE, PP, mixed plastics, construction waste, decoration waste, or bulky waste.

Input 02

Target capacity

Share the expected hourly throughput so the recommendation can stay inside the right model and module range.

Input 03

Site conditions

Available footprint, layout restrictions, voltage, and local operating limits all affect solution direction.

Input 04

Expected output

Explain the recycled products, purity goals, or final material direction you want the system to support.
