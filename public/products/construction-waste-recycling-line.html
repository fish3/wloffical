Process Overview

C&D waste flow from mixed input to reusable output

The construction waste line turns mixed, heavy, and light materials into separated output streams through crushing, screening, magnetic separation, air separation, and baling.

01

Feeding

Receive construction, decoration, demolition, or bulky waste into the integrated processing line.

02

Crushing

Reduce mixed waste into a more manageable particle size for downstream screening and separation.

03

Screening

Separate material by size to support aggregate recovery and cleaner downstream processing.

04

Magnetic separation

Recover ferrous metals and remove metal contamination from the main material stream.

05

Air separation

Separate light materials from heavier aggregates using positive-negative pressure air separation.

06

Baling

Compress recoverable light fractions or remaining materials for easier handling and logistics.

07

Output

Recycled output

Produce recycled aggregates, recovered metals, separated light materials, and other reusable fractions ready for downstream reuse.

FAQ

C&D waste recycling plant questions before project sizing

Use these answers to prepare early supplier comparison, internal feasibility review, and RFQ information for construction waste recycling equipment.

RFQ-01

What affects C&D waste recycling plant cost?

Cost depends on target capacity, feed size, required output, crusher and screening scope, magnetic separation, air separation, baling, control system, site layout, and commissioning support.

Required inputs Target capacity Site layout
RFQ-02

What materials can the construction waste line process?

The line is built for construction waste, decoration waste, demolition waste, bulky waste, and mixed light materials that need crushing, screening, metal recovery, and light-heavy separation.

Required inputs Waste composition Material photos or videos
RFQ-03

Which equipment modules are usually included?

A typical line may include feeding, crushing, trommel screening, vibrating screening, magnetic separator for C&D waste, air separation system, baler, weighing, PLC control, and remote monitoring.

Required inputs Required output specification Recovery modules needed
RFQ-04

How should buyers choose the right capacity?

Capacity should be matched to hourly input, material density, expected uptime, output specification, local transport flow, and site footprint. WEI LAN can review project data before recommending a 50-170 t/h configuration.

Required inputs Feed size Operating hours and uptime

RFQ preparation

Prepare the data package for C&D line quotation

Use this checklist to prepare enough engineering context for capacity selection, module configuration, transport planning, and installation review.

DATA-01

Waste composition

Estimate concrete, brick, wood, plastic, metal, soil, decoration waste, bulky waste, and light impurity proportions.

DATA-02

Feed size

Confirm maximum incoming size, average lump size, pre-sorting condition, and whether oversize material needs pre-crushing.

DATA-03

Target capacity

Share hourly throughput, working shifts, expected uptime, seasonal load changes, and required final aggregate specification.

DATA-04

Site layout

Provide available footprint, truck movement path, power access, water and dust-control limits, and discharge storage zones.
